The Problem

Food waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

Wasted food means wasted resources used in production.

It strains food systems and exacerbates hunger and poverty.

Reducing food waste can lead to significant environmental benefits.

Reduce

Buying only what you need reduces the likelihood of food going bad.

Meal planning helps in utilizing ingredients efficiently.

Portion control prevents excess food from being cooked.

Awareness about expiry dates aids in consuming food before it spoils.

Rescue

Food rescue involves collecting excess food from various sources.

Redirecting surplus food to shelters and food banks helps feed the hungry.

Collaborating with restaurants and supermarkets reduces food waste at the source.

Food rescue initiatives require coordination and logistics.

Redistribute

Community organizations play a vital role in redistributing surplus food.

Food banks distribute food to individuals and families in need.

Collaborating with local shelters ensures food reaches those who need it most.

Redistributing food reduces waste and helps alleviate food insecurity.
